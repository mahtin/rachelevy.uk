In 2024, I was immortalised in a bronze bust that was unveiled at a ceremony hosted by Jewish Care in London.

My sculpture was created by British portrait artist [Frances Segelman](https://segelman.com).
She's known for her commissioned works of public figures including members of the Royal Family.
Segelman produced the bust during a live sculpting session attended by my family, supporters, and representatives of Holocaust education organisations.

Frances Segelman, the artist, described me as an *inspiration*, noting that the work "*sought to capture both her dignity and her lifelong commitment to Holocaust remembrance*".
The completed bronze bust was presented as a tribute to my decades of public testimony, during which I had spoken widely about my experiences in
Auschwitz and Bergen-Belsen and my efforts to promote tolerance and understanding.
