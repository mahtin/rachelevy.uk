My name is **Rachel "Ruzena" Levy BEM** and I'm a Czechoslovak-born British Holocaust survivor, educator, and public speaker.
I'm known for my testimony about surviving Auschwitz and Bergen-Belsen, my postwar resettlement in Northern Ireland and London,
and my decades of work promoting tolerance and Holocaust remembrance.

I was born in the small mountain village of [Bhutz](/blog/bhutz/), in the Carpathian Ruthenia region of Czechoslovakia (now part of Ukraine).
In 1944, at age 14, my family and I were forced into a ghetto and subsequently deported in cattle trucks to Auschwitz-Birkenau.
I was later forced on the death march to Bergen-Belsen.
In April 1945, I was liberated by British forces.
I made it to Prague Czechoslovakia in late 1945 and reunited with my brother Chaskel, who had also survived the concentration camps.
In 1946, I was transported from Prague to [Belfast](/blog/millisle/), Northern Ireland by the Central British Fund
as part of the group of orphaned child survivors later known as *The Boys*.
Within a year I moved to London, married Phin in 1953, and raised two children.
I now have two grandchildren and one great-grandchild.

In 2019 I was [awarded](/blog/british-empire-medal/) the British Empire Medal (BEM) for services to Holocaust Education.

In 2025 I published my memoir: [*I Still Dream In Yiddish*](/blog/memoir-i-still-dream-in-yiddish/).
